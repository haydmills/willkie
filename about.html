About Willkie

Willkie Residence Center @ Indiana University Bloomington

Bringing an Academic Life into Student's Living Environment

First opened in 1965 as a traditional residence hall, Willkie Residence Center was fully remodeled into bedroom suites and apartments in 2000. The center offers a choice of single rooms with semi-private baths as well as two-bedroom and three-bedroom suites in the two high-rise towers. Apartments are located in the two low-rise sections adjacent to the center building. A hub of intellectual and social activity, and provides classrooms, rehearsal rooms, an auditorium, a library, a computer cluster, and informal lounge, conference rooms, and a fully operational convenience store. Willkie represents Indiana University’s commitment to bringing academic life into the student’s living environment.

About Wendell Willkie

Wendell Willkie

Born in Elwood, Indiana, Wendell Willkie became an accomplished attorney, businessman, and statesman. He completed his bachelor’s and law degrees at Indiana University. After serving in the U.S. Army in World War I, he practiced law in Akron, Ohio, and New York City. In 1933 he became the president of Commonwealth and Southern Corporation, a utility holding company.

In this capacity he was an outspoken opponent of the Tennessee Valley Authority. A Democrat until 1939, Willkie accepted the 1940 Republican nomination for president and was defeated by Franklin Delano Roosevelt in the election. In 1942 he traveled throughout Europe, visiting Allied countries as a special envoy for President Roosevelt. He described his experiences on this trip and his views on world peace in his book One World.
